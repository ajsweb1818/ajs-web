Special Collection (Archive)

Metal Isotopes as Markers of Biogeochemical Processes and Fluxes in the Eroding Critical Zone

This Special Collection focuses on the use of metal and silicon isotopes to trace biogeochemical processes, weathering, and material fluxes within the Critical Zone.

View Articles Back to Special Collections

Collection Overview

  • Theme: Metal and silicon isotopes in the Critical Zone
  • Published: Vol. 321, No. 8 (October 2021)
  • Format: Special Issue
  • Editor: C. Page Chamberlain

About this Collection

This Special Issue presents research on the application of isotope geochemistry to understand weathering, nutrient cycling, and biogeochemical fluxes in the Earth's Critical Zone. The studies highlight the role of erosion, vegetation, and geochemical processes in controlling elemental cycling.
